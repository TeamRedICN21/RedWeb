Medical Field Occupations

Each of our team members chose one career they would strive for if they were to be apart of the medical field.



NURSE



"Nurses have many duties, including caring for patients, communicating with doctors, administering medicine and checking vital signs. Making up for the biggest healthcare job in the U.S., nurses play a vital role in medical facilities and enjoy a large number of job opportunities. The career growth for nursing is projected to increase by 16% in the next decade, and provides a professionally and personally rewarding experience. Nurses basically makes the world a better place."

... Tyler Gholar



DOCTOR



"Doctors are often called ¨life saviors¨ because they literally are. Doctors have such an important role in life. Not only do they work all day and night but also rarely take breaks. Their main roles are to examine patients, review their medical history, diagnose illnesses or injuries, administer treatment, and counsel patients on their health and well being. Without doctors this world would literally be sick. Especially nowadays because of the pandemic"

... J'Niya Patterson



Nurse Anesthetist



"Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetists (CRNAs) have the main responsibilities of administering anesthesia and monitoring patients through their surgeries. A nurse anesthetist provides pain medication (anesthesia) care for patients before, during, and after surgery. They administer medications to keep patients asleep or pain-free during surgery and constantly monitor every biological function of the patient's body.They also have the task of ensuring that their patients have a smooth recovery after their surgery.A Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA) ensures that patients survive surgery without pain or recollection. Nurse anesthetists collaborate with surgeons, anesthesiologists, dentists, and other health care professionals to provide anesthesia to patients.While,being a nurse anesthetist is the perfect job for you. You get to be involved with the surgery and you don't need to even touch the insides of another person."

... Amber King



Hospital Desk Receptionist



"There are many people that make up hospital staff but I think that the front desk receptionist is one of the most vital parts of the staff. They have to deal with all different kinds of people all day and try to help them to the best of their abilities. They have to learn how to carefully deal with the people that come in at all times during the night and day. They have to effectively communicate with them since being in a hospital can be scary and you may not understand what is happening. Front desk receptionist are always going to be the first ones you see when coming in a hospital and they will help you as much as they can until you can see a doctor or nurse."

... Garrianna Cavett



Flight Paramedic



"My Job Title would Be an Air Medic (pilot). The reason I would love this job is simply because I love aviation and all aspects of it. My job is to carry the patient and airmed doctors to and from the hospital. I think this job fits me the best not only because I love aviation but I also enjoy helping people and making sure they are healthy and in great condition. This is what I would do and the reasons I would pursue this job in the medical field."

... Isaiah McGriggs



Orthopedic Surgeon



"If I were to become a part of the medical field, I’d choose to be an Orthopedic Surgeon. Orthopedic surgeons are those who work and diagnose bones, joints, tendons, and muscles. I’ve always had a fascination with the collaboration between bones and muscles and the way they work. I’ve experienced the pain first hand of a torn muscle and the way it heals. By becoming an ortho surgeon I’ll be able to help those who need rehabilitation and I can help little kids when their bones break."

... Joshua Reed
